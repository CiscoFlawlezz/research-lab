# NOAA

## Overview

The **National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)** is a scientific agency within the United States Department of Commerce responsible for observing, understanding, and predicting changes in the atmosphere, oceans, climate, and coasts.

NOAA develops and operates many of the United States' core environmental observing systems, numerical weather prediction models, climate archives, and public data services. Its products support weather forecasting, climate science, emergency management, transportation, agriculture, fisheries, renewable energy, and environmental research.

Within this research project, NOAA serves as a primary source of authoritative environmental data for probabilistic forecasting, prediction market analysis, and reproducible scientific research.

# Best Practices

- Preserve raw NOAA data before preprocessing.
- Record dataset versions and retrieval dates.
- Maintain UTC timestamps whenever possible.
- Archive historical forecasts for point-in-time analysis.
- Store metadata alongside observations.
- Validate spatial and temporal coverage before analysis.
- Document preprocessing and quality-control procedures.
- Distinguish observations from forecasts.
- Use reproducible data pipelines.
- Validate derived features against original datasets.
- Combine NOAA data with complementary sources when appropriate.
- Track revisions for datasets that are periodically updated.
